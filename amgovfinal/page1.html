Incumbent

The incumbent president is the current holder of office. US presidents can serve two terms, but must be elected both times. An incumbent election takes place after the president's first term, when they are seeking reelection. (Example: In the 2012 Romney vs. Obama election, Obama was the incumbent president because he was seeking reelection after his 2008-2012 term ended.)
